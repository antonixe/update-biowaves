# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Seacom Networks serves households, apartment managers, small businesses, offices, schools, clinics, and commercial buildings in and around Kapsabet, Nandi County. Visitors usually arrive with a practical question: "Can I get reliable internet here, what will it cost, and who do I call?"

Users may be comparing local providers, checking whether their building is covered, pricing a home or office connection, or bundling internet with CCTV and intercom installation. They need speed, coverage, cost, and contact paths to be obvious without reading a long marketing pitch.

## Product Purpose

This website turns local interest into qualified calls and WhatsApp conversations. It presents Seacom Networks as a reachable local ISP with fiber internet plans, security products, and a real Kapsabet office.

Success means a visitor can quickly understand the available plans, trust that support is local, confirm likely coverage, and contact the team with enough context for a site survey or installation quote.

## Brand Personality

Practical, local, and technically confident.

The brand should feel like a competent field team and network operations desk, not a generic national telco. It should communicate reliability, clear pricing, accountable support, and familiarity with the buildings and routes in Kapsabet.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI landing pages with huge soft gradients, floating orbs, glass panels, and vague "future of connectivity" language.
- Purple or blue SaaS templates that could fit any startup.
- Overly editorial luxury styling that makes a local ISP feel distant or expensive.
- Card-heavy sections where every sentence is boxed for decoration.
- Gray text on colored backgrounds with weak contrast.
- Tiny contact details hidden behind abstract conversion copy.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with local proof. Coverage, office location, phone numbers, and named buildings should appear early and clearly.
2. Make pricing scannable. Plans should read like a table or service sheet, not like lifestyle cards.
3. Use technical confidence without jargon. Mention monitoring, installation, and uptime, but keep language understandable for homes and small businesses.
4. Treat contact as the primary conversion. Phone and WhatsApp paths should be direct, visible, and repeated at the right moments.
5. Keep the system grounded. Use operational layouts, real constraints, and mapped routes instead of abstract decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible navigation. Preserve readable type on mobile, avoid text over complex imagery, respect reduced-motion preferences, and keep tap targets large enough for phone users. Do not rely on color alone to communicate plan selection, coverage status, or actions.
